💰 බෝනස් (Bonus)

In Kuwait, bonuses are not legally mandatory unless stated in the employment contract or company policy. Private sector employees may receive performance-based bonuses, while most government employees are granted an annual bonus equal to one month’s salary.

✅ Public sector “Excellence Awards” vary by job grade (up to 2,000 KWD for senior roles).
⚖️ Governed under Decision No. 17/2012 (amended by 13/2022) for incentive allowances.

🏠 අස්වැසුම (Aswasuma)

Aswasuma is a social welfare program in Sri Lanka that supports low-income families. The goal is to provide monthly financial assistance for essential needs such as food, health, and education.

🔹 Focuses on vulnerable groups (elders, disabled persons, single mothers).
🔹 Helps reduce poverty & uplift living standards.

📑 ඇග්‍රිමන්ට් (Agreements)

Employment Agreements – must clearly mention job role, salary, benefits, leave policies, and termination conditions.

Housing Lease Rules – should include contract duration, rent increments, deposits, and eviction terms under Kuwait law.

Business Contracts – partnerships, supply deals, and trade agreements must follow Kuwait commercial regulations and be legally binding.

🌍 මැදපෙරදිග ගෘහස්ථ ශ්‍රමිකයන්ගේ

Sri Lankan domestic workers in the Middle East are a key support for families back home.

🔹 Eligibility: Age 21–55, valid medical & training certificates.
🔹 Benefits: Secure salary, health coverage, remittance facilities.
🔹 Support: Sri Lanka Bureau of Foreign Employment (SLBFE) provides counseling, insurance & emergency assistance.
